Movement

The green hexes represent the allowed destinations for the spaceship.

The black arow is called "movement vector". It represents the inertia of the spaceship. In space, an object tends to keep its velocity if no force is applied to it. I wanted to represent this idea in Hexode. The black arrow points to the default destination if no move order is given to the ship this turn.

Movement is restricted by two factors, beside the movement vector.

  • ship's manoeuvrability
  • ship's max movement speed

    • To know if a hexagon is reachable (green), the hexagon must satisfies two conditions:

  • distance between the destination and the top of the movement vector must be less or equal than ship's manoeuvrability
  • distance between the ship's current position and the base of the movement vector must be less or equal than ship's max movement speed

    • Next turn, once the ship has moved to its destination, the movement vector is updated according to its new trajectory. Click on a green location to see the new ship position, its new orientation and the updated movement vector.

    Try to change manoeuvrability and max speed (they are both capped to 3, as it is a hard limit for these characteristics) and to drag and drop the tip of the movement vector to get a sense of the movement system.

    Ships going fast will be limited by their manoeuvrability, and vice versa. In some extreme cases (try with a big enough movement vector or low characteristics), you may notice than there are no valid destinations. I plan to have this situation happening in the game. It will represent a ship out of control.
